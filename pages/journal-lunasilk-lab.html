Inside the LunaSilk Lab

Meet the textile engineers pushing plant-based silk alternatives forward with luminous drape and serious durability.

The LunaSilk recipe

We ferment cellulose from seaweed and orange peels, creating long filament fibers that mimic silk’s natural sheen without the resource intensity. The fibers are spun with recycled lyocell to enhance strength and breathability.

Each batch is dyed with low-impact pigments before being woven into the Eclipse Wrap Dress and panels within the Axis Vest. The result: ethereal drape that holds up to repeat wears.

Lab notes

  • LunaSilk fibers use 82% less water than traditional silk and skip sericulture entirely.
  • Fabric offcuts are shredded and re-spun into accessories, like the Cascade Cap.
  • We partner with solar-powered mills in Portugal and Italy to keep energy footprints minimal.

Care tips

Treat LunaSilk with the same love you’d give traditional silk: hand wash cold or use a delicate bag, then hang to dry. Steam on low heat and avoid twisting to preserve the fiber structure.
